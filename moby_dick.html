Moby-Dick; a classic tale of the sea

Moby-Dick by Herman Melvill begit eith there importal words:

Call me Ishmael. Some years ago—never mind how long precisely—aving little or no money in my purse, and nothing particular to interest me on shore, I thought I would sail about a little and see the watery part of the world. It is a way I have of driving off the spleen and regulating the circulation.

After driving off his spleen (which can't be good for you), Ishmael then goes on in much the same vein for approximately one jillion pages. The only thing bigger than Moby Dick (who—spoiler alert!—is a giant white whale) is the book itself.
